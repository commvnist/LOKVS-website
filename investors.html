Investor view

Autonomy integration is the margin expansion frontier.

LOKVS is an early-stage, Canadian R&D venture focused on a high-need wedge: UWB-based mobile relative localization delivered as a complete subsystem stack rather than a single hardware product.

Investment logic

Category timing

UWB adoption is expanding in automation and precision localization and remains adjacent to fast-growing robotics and industrial programs.

Technical defensibility

The advantage compounds across firmware behavior, topology handling, and integration interfaces—not just component sourcing.

Commercial wedge

Clear buyer segments: systems integrators and autonomy manufacturers that are already paying integration tax.

Go-to-market and early revenue shape

Early-stage model: design partners, not volume-first hardware sales.

  • Design partner pilots with robotics OEMs and defense/autonomy integrators.
  • Milestone-driven programs tied to bring-up, integration stability, and field validation.
  • Expansion path into additional sensing/perception modules under the same platform contract model.

Positioning signal: a subsystem supplier whose value increases as customers scale platform complexity and increase their need for deterministic integration behavior.

Source context

Relevant market and systems context used in our thesis includes: MarketsandMarkets, UWB Alliance, McKinsey complexity paper.
